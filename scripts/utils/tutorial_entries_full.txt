--- TutorialList_001_Title ---
Blood Collector

--- TutorialList_001_Description_00 ---
Use the blood collector to gather
<COLOR preset="TextColor0">infected blood</COLOR> which can be
harnessed to craft useful items.

--- TutorialList_001_Description_01 ---
You can increase your crafting capabilities
by <COLOR preset="TextColor0">analyzing blood specimens
with the laser microscope</COLOR>.

--- TutorialList_002_Title ---
Using Typewriters

--- TutorialList_002_Description_00 ---
When playing on Standard (Classic) difficulty
or above, <COLOR preset="TextColor0">autosaves during Grace's story
will be limited.</COLOR> Ink ribbons are required
to save at typewriters.

--- TutorialList_002_Description_01 ---
<COLOR preset="TextColor0">Ink ribbons</COLOR> are scarce, so make sure
to use them wisely.

--- TutorialList_003_Title ---
Weapon Shortcuts

--- TutorialList_003_Description ---
Weapons can be assigned to shortcuts
via the Items menu. Up to six weapons
can be assigned at once, two weapons
in three different directions.

--- TutorialList_004_Title ---
Hemolytic Injectors

--- TutorialList_004_Description_00 ---
If you sneak up on an enemy from behind,
you can use a hemolytic injector to take
them out instantly—the same strategy
can be used on staggered enemies.

--- TutorialList_005_Title ---
Custom Parts

--- TutorialList_005_Description ---
Custom parts can be utilized to enhance
certain weapons.

Select custom parts via the Items menu
to attach them to a compatible weapon.

--- TutorialList_008_Title ---
Managing Credits

--- TutorialList_008_Description_00 ---
The Tactical Tracker logs your combat
data in real time: the more enemies you
dispatch, the more credits you earn.
<COLOR preset="TextColor0">Credits can be used to purchase items,
weapons, or upgrades at a supply box</COLOR>.

--- TutorialList_011_Title ---
Quick Turning

--- TutorialList_011_Description ---
Use <ICON QUICK_TURN> to perform a quick turn.

--- TutorialList_012_Title ---
Parrying

--- TutorialList_012_Description_00 ---
You can parry certain enemy attacks
with <ICON GUARD>. But be careful, you can't
parry with a blunt hatchet.

--- TutorialList_012_Description_01 ---
Time it just right, and you can perform
a perfect parry that will stagger
your enemies.

--- TutorialList_013_Title ---
Sharpening the Hatchet

--- TutorialList_013_Description ---
Stay sharp. If the hatchet's durability
reaches zero, the blade will dull and
become unusable.
Use <ICON SHARPEN> or <ICON MELEE_WEAPON> (when blunt)
to regain the edge in battle.

--- TutorialList_014_Title ---
Close Combat Attacks

--- TutorialList_014_Description ---
While wielding the hatchet, you can
use <ICON MELEE_WEAPON> to perform close combat
attacks. You can perform a more
powerful attack with <ICON GUARD_ATTACK>.

--- TutorialList_015_Title ---
The Map

--- TutorialList_015_Description ---
View the map with <ICON SUBMENU> to confirm your current
position, destination, and item locations.

--- TutorialList_017_Title ---
Restoring Health

--- TutorialList_017_Description ---
Use green herbs or med injectors to restore
health. Green herbs aren't as potent on
their own, so try using them in crafting
to increase their healing properties.

--- TutorialList_018_Title ---
Melee Attacks

--- TutorialList_018_Description_00 ---
Attacking an enemy's head or legs will stagger
them. While staggered, you can knock them
back with a melee attack using <ICON INTERACT_MARTIAL_ARTS>.
Use this tactic to your advantage to
knock back multiple nearby enemies.

--- TutorialList_018_Description_01 ---
Knock an enemy onto the ground or into
a wall with a melee attack, and you'll
have a short window of time to perform
a finishing attack with <ICON INTERACT_MELEE_WEAPON>.

--- TutorialList_019_Title ---
Managing Items

--- TutorialList_019_Description ---
Item management is key to survival. Avoid
throwing items away by storing items in
the item box or utilizing a hip pouch to
increase your carrying capacity.

--- TutorialList_020_Title ---
Selling Weapons

--- TutorialList_020_Description ---
Weapons that don't fit into your attaché
case can be sold at the supply box.
They can be bought back for the same
amount, so just consider them on hold
for safekeeping.

--- TutorialList_021_Title ---
The Items Menu

--- TutorialList_021_Description ---
You can check your acquired items from
the Items menu and examine them for
more details.

--- TutorialList_022_Title ---
Using Enemy Weapons

--- TutorialList_022_Description ---
Enemy weapons can be picked up and thrown
at other enemies to stagger them, but only
once a target is in range.

--- TutorialList_023_Title ---
Light-Sensitive Zombies

--- TutorialList_023_Description ---
Among the infected, there are those which
exhibit an aversion to light. They will
compulsively turn off any lights around
them, so use this to lure them where you
want them.

--- TutorialList_024_Title ---
Sound-Sensitive Zombies

--- TutorialList_024_Description ---
Certain infected are extremely sensitive
to sound, and will fly into a rage when they
hear loud noises. But since they will attack
the source of sound indiscriminately, they
may even attack other infected.

--- TutorialList_025_Title ---
Antique Coins

--- TutorialList_025_Description ---
Antique coins can be exchanged
for rare items in the parlor.

--- TutorialList_026_Title ---
Knife Combat

--- TutorialList_026_Description_00 ---
While wielding knives, you can slash at
an enemy with <ICON MELEE_WEAPON>, or perform a quick thrust
with <ICON GUARD_ATTACK>.

--- TutorialList_026_Description_01 ---
When in doubt, it's always good to have
a knife on hand. Knives can be used
to escape when an enemy catches you
in its grasp—but you won't be able to
get your knife back until you defeat it.

--- TutorialList_027_Title ---
Stealth Kills

--- TutorialList_027_Description ---
Use the hatchet to execute unsuspecting
enemies from behind.

--- TutorialList_028_Title ---
Blister Head Mutation

--- TutorialList_028_Description_00 ---
Infected enemies that have been put down once
may mutate and rise again. This mutation
causes abnormal growth, starting at the head.
As such, they have been given the name
"Blister Heads."

--- TutorialList_028_Description_01 ---
There are two sure-fire ways to prevent
the infected from mutating into Blister
Heads: destroy their heads in battle or
use a hemolytic injector to break down
their remains.

--- TutorialList_030_Title ---
Item Check Marks

--- TutorialList_030_Description ---
Items with check marks have served
their purpose. You can discard them
to free up space in your inventory.

--- TutorialList_031_Title ---
Blister Borne

--- TutorialList_031_Description_00 ---
The only way to defeat the large, mutated
Blister Borne is by destroying the tumors
covering its body.

--- TutorialList_031_Description_01 ---
When a tumor bursts, it'll cause all
the surrounding zombies to mutate.
Either burst the tumors where there are
no other zombies, or destroy the heads
of the zombies before they can change.

--- TutorialList_032_Title ---
Elite Guards

--- TutorialList_032_Description ---
The elite guards won't hold back if
they spot you. Stay hidden and watch
your opponents' movements carefully.

--- TutorialList_033_Title ---
The Commander

--- TutorialList_033_Description ---
Novice tactics will have little effect
against the highly-trained commander.
Use parries and powerful attacks to make
yourself an opening.

--- TutorialList_034_Title ---
Licker β2

--- TutorialList_034_Description ---
Since β2 type lickers cannot see, they
hone in on their prey using sound alone.
Watch your step and try not to make too
much noise...or you might end up on
the business end of one of their tongues.

--- TutorialList_035_Title ---
Leon's Coughing Fits

--- TutorialList_035_Description ---
Leon is reaching his limit. Sudden bouts
of coughing and blurred vision will cause
him to falter. He can only wait for these
effects to subside.

--- TutorialList_038_Title ---
Charms (Leon)

--- TutorialList_038_Description ---
You can equip a charm to your weapon
to activate an effect. Equip a charm by
selecting a weapon via the Items menu,
then select the charm you want to equip.

--- TutorialList_039_Title ---
Second Playthrough (Leon)

--- TutorialList_039_Description ---
From the second playthrough onward, Leon can
obtain and tune up equipment earlier.
- The Tactical Tracker begins in his inventory.
- Supply boxes are available in each area.
- Equipment acquired in previous playthroughs
can be obtained from the supply box.

--- TutorialList_040_Title ---
Camera Wobble Settings

--- TutorialList_040_Description ---
Camera wobble intensity can be adjusted
via Options.

--- TutorialList_041_Title ---
Exploration Tips

--- TutorialList_041_Description ---
Exploration and information will be the key
to your survival. Check the map regularly.

--- TutorialList_042_Title ---
Improving Your Aim

--- TutorialList_042_Description ---
If you aim without moving your weapon,
the reticle will narrow. This will increase
the power of shots and make them more
likely to stagger enemies.

--- TutorialList_043_Title ---
Finishing Attacks (Firearms)

--- TutorialList_043_Description ---
A firearm finishing attack is a shot at
point-blank range that lets you instantly
take down an enemy with low health.

--- TutorialList_045_Title ---
The Girl

--- TutorialList_045_Description ---
The Girl detests light. If you hear
her coming, get to a bright room.

--- TutorialList_046_Title ---
Clearing the Path

--- TutorialList_046_Description ---
You need to take down enough enemies to clear
an escape route. Determine which enemies pose
the most trouble.

--- TutorialList_047_Title ---
Targeting Attacks

--- TutorialList_047_Description ---
Aim for the head or legs to stagger an enemy.
Once staggered, you can send them flying
using a melee attack with <ICON INTERACT_MARTIAL_ARTS>.

--- TutorialList_048_Title ---
Higher Blood Yields

--- TutorialList_048_Description ---
An enemy's head and legs will bleed more
than other parts after destroying them, so
aim for those areas to collect more blood
after taking them out.

--- TutorialList_049_Title ---
Zombies and Flashlights

--- TutorialList_049_Description ---
Zombies won't change their behavior
in response to flashlights.

--- TutorialList_050_Title ---
Requiem

--- TutorialList_050_Description ---
Use Requiem if you find yourself in trouble.

--- TutorialList_051_Title ---
Choosing Weapons

--- TutorialList_051_Description ---
Use the right weapon for the situation.
Shotguns are great for close range
attacks with high firepower.

--- TutorialList_052_Title ---
Chunks

--- TutorialList_052_Description ---
Due to the amount of cushioning fat on their
bodies, many attacks won't hurt the Chunks.
Aim for their heads and try to stagger them
to give yourself an opening.

--- TutorialList_053_Title ---
Gas Canisters

--- TutorialList_053_Description ---
Shoot gas canisters to engulf the
surrounding area in an explosion.

--- TutorialList_054_Title ---
Beware of Flying Objects

--- TutorialList_054_Description ---
Make sure to avoid flying debris. Consider
your position carefully and quickly take
out the zombies throwing rubble.

--- TutorialList_055_Title ---
BSAA Zombies

--- TutorialList_055_Description ---
Zombies wielding machine guns pose
a severe threat. Once they open fire,
find cover until they have to reload.

--- TutorialList_056_Title ---
Mortars

--- TutorialList_056_Description ---
Get under cover when the mortars start
firing. You'll have to take out the zombie
controlling the weapon to stop it.

--- TutorialList_057_Title ---
Breaking Glass

--- TutorialList_057_Description ---
Glass surfaces are incredibly fragile and
require extra caution. However, shooting
the glass out from under an enemy will
cause them to fall through.

--- TutorialList_058_Title ---
Tyrant T-501

--- TutorialList_058_Description ---
You can't fight the Tyrant properly in tight
spaces. Your only option is to run.

--- TutorialList_060_Title ---
Plant 43

--- TutorialList_060_Description ---
Shooting Plant 43 while its flowers are closed
won't be very effective, so wait until they open
to unleash your attacks.

--- TutorialList_061_Title ---
Victor (Mutated)

--- TutorialList_061_Description ---
The best defense is a good offense.
Shoot Victor before he can blast you
with his rocket launcher.

--- TutorialList_062_Title ---
Victor (Rampant)

--- TutorialList_062_Description ---
Victor's weak points will be exposed when
he starts thrashing around. Aim for them
to put an end to the fight.

--- TutorialList_063_Title ---
Bottles of Acid

--- TutorialList_063_Description ---
If you break a bottle of acid, the contents
will disperse into the surrounding area.
A particularly effective strategy against
the Licker β2.

--- TutorialList_066_Title ---
Saving

--- TutorialList_066_Description ---
Use typewriters to save your progress.

--- TutorialList_067_Title ---
The Item Box

--- TutorialList_067_Description ---
There's a limit to how many items you can
carry around, so if you don't want to leave
anything behind, store items you don't need
in the item box.

--- TutorialList_068_Title ---
Body Armor

--- TutorialList_068_Description ---
If you find yourself taking more damage
than you bargained for, you might want
to buy some body armor. Worn out body
armor can be repaired at the supply box.

--- TutorialList_069_Title ---
Super Tyrant Weakness

--- TutorialList_069_Description ---
Even the Super Tyrant has a weakness.
Try targeting its exposed heart.

--- TutorialList_070_Title ---
Item Crafting

--- TutorialList_070_Description ---
You can craft items by combining materials
together via the Crafting menu, or by
selecting the material and then "Combine"
via the Items menu.
